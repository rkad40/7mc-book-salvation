# The Covenant That Could Not Save 

> #### 📄 Abstract
> This transitional chapter marks the conclusion of the four major arguments that “works of law” in Romans refers to works of the Mosaic Law rather than good works in general. It prepares the reader to move from the positive Catholic case into a series of Protestant objections and Catholic replies. The central question remains whether Paul is refuting works-salvation in the abstract or the Judaizer claim that Gentiles must come under the Mosaic covenant to be saved.
